"Is my voice baritone or tenor?" (John 57)

You could be a baritone capable of singing in the tenor range, or vice versa. Practically speaking, at least in opera, you are a tenor if you can sing tenor roles, including the top notes. Otherwise you are a baritone.

What are the Main types of Female and Male voices?

    Females:
  • The soprano:The soprano is the highest singing voice. The typical soprano voice lies between C4 (middle C) and C6 (high C).
  • Mezzo-soprano:The mezzo-soprano voice is the middle-range voice type for females. It lies between the soprano and contralto ranges, over-lapping both of them. The typical range of this voice is between A3 (the A below middle C) to A5 (two octaves higher).
  • Contralto:The contralto voice is the lowest female voice. A The typical contralto range lies between F3 (the F below middle C) to F5 (the second F above middle C).
      • Males:
    • Tenor: The tenor is the highest male voice within the modal register. Tenor voice range (C3–C5) indicated on piano keyboard in green with dot marking middle C (C4). The typical tenor voice lies between C3 (one octave below middle C) to C5 (one octave above middle C).
    • Baritone: The baritone voice is the middle-range voice type for males; it lies between the bass and tenor ranges, overlapping both of them. Baritone voice range (A2–A4) indicated on piano keyboard in green with dot marking middle C (C4). Baritone comes from the Italian baritono, with its Greek root word barytonos, "deep-voiced," combining barys, "heavy or deep" and tonos, "tone.
